INTERESTED IN:


Quantum Mechanics

Quantum mechanics is the study of matter and its interactions with energy on the scale of atomic and subatomic particles. Classical physics is still used in much of modern science and technology. However, towards the end of the 19th century, scientists discovered phenomena in both the large (macro) and the small (micro) worlds that classical physics could not explain, which led to the development of quantum mechanics

In quantum mechanics, there are no 'particles' or 'waves'; the quantum state of everything is represented using complex valued mathematical functions called wavefunctions. The predictions of quantum mechanics are formed from the mathematical calculations regarding the interactions of these wavefunctions and potentials. Most of the behaviors of particles in quantum level are very strange and far from common sense, yet it completely align with the exprimental observations. That's why Richard Feynman once said: "I hope you can accept the nature as She is -Absurd!"

Computational Physics

The creativity of human beings have no limitations, but our computational ability is very low. In general, we are only capable to compute simple mathematical solutions for nearly ideal case of the physics problems. But in the real world where physics is actually applied, the systems and their functions are much more complex.

So we use computers for that purpose mainly by taking advantage of their ability to perform large number of iterations. Computational physics is the study of scientific problems using computational methods; it combines computer science, physics and applied mathematics to develop scientific solutions to complex problems. Computational physics complements the areas of theory and experimentation in traditional scientific investigation.

Electronics

Electronics is a scientific and engineering discipline that studies and applies the principles of physics to design, create, and operate devices that manipulate electrons and other electrically charged particles. It focuses on using active devices such as transistors, diodes, and integrated circuits to control and amplify the flow of electric current and to convert it from one form to another, such as from alternating current (AC) to direct current (DC) or from analog to digital.

It has made significant impact in our day-to-day life. From the small gadgets with us to the latest advancemets of tecnology are implemented through electronic devices. Electronics industry is much bigger than we think as its developments highly depend on various branches including nanotechnology, solid state physics and quantum mechanics.
